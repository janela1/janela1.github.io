Who is this course for?

Put simply, this course is designed for anyone! While it falls under the COMP discipline, you do not need to be a CS major nor do you need to possess any prior coding skills. This course emphasizes primarily coding, but also teaches you principles of good design.

Make sure you note that this course is strictly an elective though; it does not contribute to the CS major or fulfill a general education requirement. That said, there are no prequisite requirements to enroll in COMP126!

Who is the instructor?

COMP126 is taught by Dr. Tessa Joseph-Nicholas. She currently serves as an Associate Teaching Professor, Director of Digital Arts and Humanities Projects, and Diversity Liaison to the College of Arts and Sciences at the Department of Computer Science at UNC-Chapel Hill.

Dr. Joseph-Nicholas has her PhD in English and Comparative Literature and her MFA in Creative Writing/Poetry! As a side note, if you have any interest in computers in culture or poetry writing, Dr. Joseph-Nicholas is a great resource to talk to!

Where do we meet?

This course meets in an Interactive Classroom in Sitterson Hall, room 011. The header at the top of this site shows you what that looks like! We meet on Tuesdays and Thursdays from 12:30pm-1:45pm.

If you would like to meet with Dr. Joseph-Nicholas outside of class time, she holds office hours in the FB portion of Sitterson, room 114 on Thursdays from 11am-12:15pm. If this time doesn't work for you, check out the instructor contact info at the top righthand side of this page to schedule an appointment!
